ROBO PICK PLACE

Navigate your robot to pick and place objects in a challenging environment.

Theme

Design a robot (wired or wireless) within the specified dimensions that can operate manually and navigate complex paths. The robot that completes the task in the least amount of time while earning maximum points will be declared the winner.

Maximum Team Size

Up to 5 members per team.

Guidelines

  • Robot dimensions must not exceed 30cm x 30cm x 30cm (L x B x H).
  • Robot may be wired or wireless. Wired bots must have slack wires during the run.
  • Maximum weight allowed: 5 kg.
  • Standard AC power (220V, 50Hz) will be provided. Participants must bring their own adapters or batteries.
  • No LEGO or ready-made kits allowed. Violations result in disqualification.
  • Robot must be electrically powered only. IC engines are not permitted.
  • Battery type must be sealed (gel cell, lithium, NiCad, dry cell, etc.).
  • Voltage must not exceed 20V DC at any point in the machine.

Event Rules

  • The robot must pick up and place items in specified spots with desired accuracy.
  • Teams can be from different institutes, and all members must carry institute ID cards.
  • The robot must not damage the arena or lose any parts during the run.
  • Time-trial based competition. No extra time or re-runs will be granted.
  • Organizers are not responsible for any injuries or loss during the event.
  • The decision of the judges is final and binding.
  • Organizers reserve the right to modify rules. Participants should stay updated via the website.

Track Specifications

  • Pickable item: Wooden Cube (3.5cm x 3.5cm x 3.5cm) with a hook on top.
  • Track may include uneven surfaces and obstacles.
  • Obstacles: switch bridge, speed breakers, marble pit, slippery path, rotating disc, ramp, seesaw, humps, sand, etc.
  • Exact design and size will be revealed during the event.

Rounds

  • Qualifying Round
  • Final Round

Judgement Criteria

  • Task completion (pick and place)
  • Number of checkpoints cleared
  • Total time taken

Disqualification Criteria

  • Using non-approved parts or tools.
  • Plagiarism or copying of robot design.
  • Failure to maintain team spirit and discipline.
